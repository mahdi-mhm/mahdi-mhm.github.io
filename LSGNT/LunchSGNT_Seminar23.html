LSGNT Lunch Seminar

The L-unch-SGNT Seminar is a weekly seminar organised to encourage discussion between first year LSGNT students and potential supervisors from UCL, KCL, and Imperial. Each week there will be two talks (one from a geometer and one from a number theorist), in which each speaker will discuss their research and potential projects. Students are encouraged to ask questions and discuss ideas between and after the talks.

The seminar will run in a hybrid format on Tuesdays 12pm-2pm for 11 weeks starting the 3rd of October. The seminar will be held in room MB1.3 in the Macadam Building, KCL, and food will be provided.

For the online access to the seminar, please contact Mahdi Haghshenas.

If you are interested in giving a talk, please fill in the form, and we will contact you as soon as possible.

This seminar is organized by Mahdi Haghshenas and Yicheng Yang, and funded by the LSGNT.

10/10 13:15 Dmitri Panov Spherical surfaces Slides

Abstract

A spherical surface is a surface that can be glued from a collection of spherical triangles. It has a curvature one metric outside finite number of conical singularities. Using simple examples, I'll explain how the theory of spherical surfaces is connected to enumerative geometry (Schubert calculus) and values of Riemann zeta function (at negative integers).
17/10 12:00 Jean Lagacé Spectral geometry, minimal surfaces and homogenisation

Abstract

In this talk, I will explain the relationship between minimal surfaces (closed and with boundary) and shape optimisation for eigenvalues of elliptic operators, such as the Laplacian or the Dirichlet-to-Neumann map. I will also explain how we can use homogenisation theory, a branch of applied mathematics, to a geometric setting in order to construct free boundary minimal surfaces with a particularly large area. By the end of this talk we will see how disparate tools from varied areas such as geometric analysis, spectral theory and applied analysis can come together in the study of minimal surfaces. This is joint work with A. Girouard (Laval), A. Henrot (Nancy) and M. Karpukhin (UCL)

14/11 12:00 Davoud Cheraghi Rotation domains, nearly rotation domains, and their geometry

Abstract

A rotation domain (also a Siegel disk) of a holomorphic map on a complex manifold is an invariant domain on which the map is biholomrphically conjugate to a rotation on an open set in C^n. While there are strong local results about their existence, there is little known about the global complex geometry of rotation domains. We shall discuss some old and new results on rotation domains.

21/11 13:15 Anthea Monod Introducing algebraic topology and geometry in statistics and machine learning

Abstract

I will also overview some of the work I do with my group in these fields that emphasizes feasibility, interpretability, and computational efficiency, especially applied to real data. Finally, I will close with some open research questions I am currently working on, in particular some ideas for mini projects.
28/11 12:00 Rachel Newton Diophantine equations, local-global principles and arithmetic statistics

Abstract

Let X be an algebraic variety over the rationals. If X has a rational point then it has a point over the reals and over the p-adics for all primes p, i.e. a point over every completion of the rationals. The Hasse principle holds if the existence of points over the reals and over the p-adics for all primes p guarantees the existence of a rational point. This talk will discuss the Brauer-Manin obstruction, which explains some failures of the Hasse principle, and also how often the Hasse principle fails in some families of varieties. This will then lead on to some related questions in arithmetic statistics.
