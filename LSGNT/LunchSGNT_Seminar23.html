LSGNT Lunch Seminar

The L-unch-SGNT Seminar is a weekly seminar organised to encourage discussion between first year LSGNT students and potential supervisors from UCL, KCL, and Imperial. Each week there are will be two talks (one from a geometer and one from a number theorist), in which each speaker will discuss their research and potential projects. Students are encouraged to ask questions and discuss ideas between and after the talks.

The seminar will run in a hybrid format on Tuesdays 12pm-2pm for 11 weeks starting the 3rd of October. The seminar will be held in Room MB1.3 in the Macadam Building, KCL, and food will be provided.

For the online access to the seminar, please contact Mahdi Haghshenas.

This seminar is organized by Mahdi Haghshenas and Yicheng Yang, and funded by the LSGNT.

03/10 12:00 Peter Jossen The period conjecture

Abstract

Periods, in the context of this talk, are complex the numbers obtained by integrating algebraic differential forms on algebraic varieties defined over Q. The period conjecture is a conjecture about the transcendence nature of these numbers. I will formulate the conjecture and give some examples.

03/10 13:15 Paolo Cascini Higher Dimensional Geometry

Abstract

I will give a short survey about the Minimal Model Program and some of its applications.

Abstract

In this talk I will describe various p-adic notions of integration or linear differential equations. I will also describe some applications, old and new, to understanding questions about rational points, torsion points or other forms of 'unlikely intersections' on algebraic varieties.
10/10 13:15 Dmitri Panov Spherical surfaces

Abstract

A spherical surface is a surface that can be glued from a collection of spherical triangles. It has a curvature one metric outside finite number of conical singularities. Using simple examples, I'll explain how the theory of spherical surfaces is connected to enumerative geometry (Schubert calculus) and values of Riemann zeta function (at negative integers).
