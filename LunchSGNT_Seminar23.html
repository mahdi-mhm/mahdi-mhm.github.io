LSGNT Lunch Seminar

The Lunch Seminar is a weekly seminar organised to encourage discussion between first year LSGNT students and potential supervisors from UCL, KCL, and Imperial. Each week there are going to be two talks (one from a geometer and one from a number theorist), in which each speaker will discuss their research and potential projects.

Students are encouraged to ask questions and discuss ideas between and after the talks.

The seminar will run in a hybrid format on Tuesdays 12pm-2pm for 11 weeks starting the 3th of October. The seminar will be held in Room MB.13 in the Macadam Building, KCL. Food will be provided.

For the online access to the seminar, please reach out to Mahdi Haghshenas.

This seminar is organized by Mahdi Haghshenas and Yicheng Yang, and funded by the LSGNT.

03/10 12:00 Peter Jossen The period conjecture Slides

Abstract

Periods, in the context of this talk, are complex the numbers obtained by integrating algebraic differential forms on algebraic varieties defined over Q. The period conjecture is a conjecture about the transcendence nature of these numbers. I will formulate the conjecture and give some examples.
